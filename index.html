Dr. Dian Fossey

The woman who gave her life to save the gorillas

Dian Fossey with gorillas (THE ATLANTA JOURNAL-CONSTITUTION)
When you realize the value of all life, you dwell less on what is past and concentrate more on the preservation of the future.
- Dian Fossey
  • 1932: Born on January 16, 1932, in San Francisco, California.
  • 1954: Graduated in occupational therapy from San Jose State College and worked as a hospital intern in California.
  • 1955: Became director of the Kosair Crippled Children's Hospital's occupational therapy department in Louisville, Kentucky.
  • 1963: Embarked on a trip to Africa visiting Kenya, Tanzania, Zimbabwe and the Congo, among other areas.
  • 1966: Accepted an offer to take on a long-term study of the endangered gorillas and lived among them in the Democratic Republic of Congo until civil war forced her to escape to Rwanda.
  • 1967: Established the Karisoke Research Foundation in Rwanda's Volcanoes National Park to facilitate the study of mountain gorillas.
  • 1976: Obtained a Ph.D. based on her research at Cambridge University.
  • 1983: Published the book Gorillas in the Mist which became a best-seller.
  • 1985: Was murdered, presumably by poachers, at her Rwandan forest camp.
